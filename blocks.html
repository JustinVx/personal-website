Blocks is a series of interactive challenges related to an online course, that have to be achieved with the help of a team. Together you score points, which can result in prizes. In the process you get a better understanding about the content of the course and make new friends.

The assignment

EdX is a website where people can follow online courses from some of the best universities in the world. These courses are called Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) and are available for free. The Delft University of Technology started their first two courses and found out that after the first weeks most of the students drop out. Their question is how to prevent this from happening!

Research

In order to find out what is happening I observed and interviewed students that went to a ‘real’ lecture, that was also available online. I also interviewed a few students that were enrolled in one of the MOOCs. There were two main insights that I gained from the research. First of all, students are easily bored or frustrated when learning something new. The other insight is that interactions between students are valuable and help in making both the experience of learning more pleasant and also make it easier to learn.

Design goal

Make people feel part of a team while following courses on edX individually.

Building the concept

In their programs, many schools and universities are focusing more and more on so called active learning or learning by doing. Research shows that through projects, workshops and experiments students are better at understanding and recalling what they learned and consider the learning experience to be more enjoyable. The main reason that these developments are not seen in online education is because there is no good solution to make students work together. I wanted to change that.

I contacted the faculty of Chemical Engineering (at the Delft University of Technology) and developed a concept for an online experiment on the topic of mass transfer over two phases. In an iterative process I performed a number of tests with students to test how they can work together on this experiment using online communication. All the conclusions, such as the importance of a challenge and the need for variety were used in developing the final concept.
