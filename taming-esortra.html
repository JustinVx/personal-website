Taming Esortra.

Taming Esortra is a persuasive game for people with osteoarthritis. Osteoarthritis is a common joint disease with joint stiffness and pain as the most important symptoms; making it hard and painful for patients to exercise. However, exercise is important and can actually stop the disease from getting worse. Taming Esortra uses big data, narrative game-elements and self-monitoring to help patients gain control over their disease. With the help of Taming Esortra the user can gain insight in their personal burden caused by osteoarthritis and see how they can decrease that burden through exercise. The product was created as part of my graduation thesis and rewarded with a 9 out of 10.

This project included:

  • A collaboration with the Biomedical Imaging Group Rotterdam (BIGR) at the Erasmus University Medical Centre.
  • A collaboration with healthcare start-up 1Minuut Zorginnovatie.
  • Extensive literature research on osteoarthritis, healthcare, eHealth, behavior change theories and gamification.
  • Interviews with experts on osteoarthritis.
  • The identification of several core mechanisms that resulted from insights gained during the research.
  • The creation of a design vision and a design goal.
  • An ideation phase and the elaboration of three detailed concepts. Followed by further development of the chosen concept.
  • A narrative and narrative game-elements based on Campbell’s concept of the Hero’s Journey.
  • A working prototype of an iPhone app.
  • User tests with people that are diagnosed with osteoarthritis; resulting in eighteen findings in five categories.
  • Ten recommendations on how to move forward with all the knowledge gained during the project.
